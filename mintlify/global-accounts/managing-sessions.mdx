A session represents a short-lived authorization window for signed Global Account actions.

Sessions are created when a customer verifies a registered credential. The session signing key is encrypted to the client device and used to sign action payloads, such as quote execution from a Global Account.

## When to manage sessions

List or revoke sessions when you need to:

- Show active devices or signed-in sessions to a customer
- Sign a customer out of a device
- Revoke access after account recovery
- Reduce risk after suspicious activity

## Revocation requires a signed action

Session revocation can require the same signed-retry pattern as other sensitive account actions. The customer authenticates, signs the revocation payload, and your backend retries the request with `Grid-Wallet-Signature`.
